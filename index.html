What it means
to say a name correctly

Our names contain stories of heritage, family, and personal identity.

We carry them with us everywhere we go.

So when someone messes them up, it’s harmful.

Not making an effort to pronounce someone’s name is a sign of disrespect.

It’s alienating, and indicates that you don’t acknowledge them as individuals.

This is not to say you have to automatically know how to pronounce everyone’s name.

The important thing is demonstrating that you care enough to try.

And as a practical matter, it’s not always possible to do this.

But among coworkers, peers, and students, we have the ability

— and moreover, the duty —

to put in the work to get it right.
